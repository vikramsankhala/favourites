Step by Step Workshop Manual
8-Phase Build Process
Phase 01 — Pre-Swap Assessment & Sourcing
Know Before
You Buy
Before spending money on a donor engine, verify your Verito's condition and confirm you're buying the right K9K variant. The K9K family spans 65–115 PS — the engine code stamped on the block is your proof of identity.
  • 01Inspect the Verito subframe and engine bay for rust, bent mounts, or prior accident damage. A bent subframe means the engine will not sit level.
  • 02Source a K9K engine with code 796 (Dacia Logan 86PS) or 884 (Duster 90PS). Both are direct fitments. Physically verify the three-digit code stamped on the block above the starter motor.
  • 03Request a compression test on the donor engine before purchase. All four cylinders must read 25–35 bar. A reading below 22 bar means worn rings — walk away.
  • 04Confirm the injection system type: Delphi (grey injector bodies) or Bosch (black). You will need matching C2I codes and potentially matching ECU for Bosch units.
  • 05In India: notify your RTO. Engine number changes require Form 29/30, RC book endorsement, and insurer notification. Doing this post-swap avoids legal complications during inspection.

Phase 02 — Draining, Disconnecting & Prep
Drain, Label
& Disconnect
Systematic disconnection prevents broken connectors, crossed wires, and missed fasteners. Label every sensor plug, hose, and bracket before removal. Photograph the engine bay from multiple angles.
  • 01Lift the car, support on four axle stands at sill jacking points. Remove engine undertray (4× M8 bolts). Place oil drain pan — remove sump plug (M14, 25 Nm) and drain fully.
  • 02Open the coolant bleed nipple on the thermostat housing. Remove the bottom radiator hose to drain the cooling system into a clean container for reuse.
  • 03Disconnect the battery — negative terminal first, then positive. Tape the terminal so it cannot accidentally touch metalwork during the work.
  • 04Disconnect and label every electrical connector: ECU multi-plug (usually 2 large connectors on the bulkhead), MAF sensor, MAP/boost sensor, coolant temperature sensor, oil pressure switch, crank position sensor, cam position sensor, glow plug relay, and all 4 injector plugs.
  • 05Remove the air intake ducting from the airbox to the turbo inlet. Remove the intercooler-to-throttle pipe (the pressure pipe). Remove the EGR valve hose connections and the EGR cooler coolant hoses (if fitted).
  • 06Disconnect the turbo oil feed pipe (banjo bolt, M12, 20 Nm) and the turbo oil return hose at the sump. Cap both to prevent oil spillage.
  • 07Loosen the exhaust downpipe from the turbo outlet flange (3 or 4 × M8 studs + nuts). Use copper anti-seize spray on the studs before attempting removal — these corrode badly.

Pro Tip Spray WD-40 or penetrating oil on all exhaust manifold nuts and turbo inlet clamps the night before. Exhaust fasteners are the number one cause of delays.
Phase 03 — Gearbox Separation & Driveshafts
Separate the
Powertrain
The K9K is mated to the JH3 5-speed gearbox. Separate the gearbox from the engine block before hoisting. On the Verito's transverse layout, both driveshafts must come out before the engine/gearbox assembly can be lifted.
  • 01Drain the gearbox oil (M18 plug under the gearbox, 2.0L of GL4 75W-80). Support the gearbox on the transmission jack before removing mounts.
  • 02Remove the gear selector linkage rod and the reverse lamp switch connector from the gearbox.
  • 03Undo the left (driver's side) engine mount bracket. The K9K Verito has three mounts: right-side torque strut, left-side gearbox mount, and a lower rear mount on the subframe.
  • 04Remove the right front driveshaft: undo the hub nut (M24, 230 Nm — use an impact wrench), remove the lower ball joint pinch bolt (M12, 60 Nm), pull the hub carrier outward and slide the driveshaft out of the gearbox using the CV puller/slide hammer.
  • 05Remove the left driveshaft the same way. Note: the left-side intermediate shaft is supported by a bracket bolted to the engine block — remove the bracket (3× M10 bolts) to free it.
  • 06With both shafts clear, disconnect the 6 bell-housing bolts (M10 and M12, various torques). Slide the gearbox away from the engine by ~10cm and lower onto a wooden block or leave supported on the transmission jack.

Phase 04 — Engine Removal & Bench Comparison
Hoist Out &
Compare Blocks
With the gearbox separated, the engine is free on three mounts. Attach the engine crane, remove the final mount bolts, and lift the engine straight up and out. Lay the old and new engine side by side on a bench or clean floor.
  • 01Attach the engine crane chain/sling to the K9K lift brackets (cast into the head and block). Raise the crane until the chain is taut — no slack.
  • 02Remove the final three engine mount bolts (right torque mount, left bracket, subframe lower mount). These will be 2 × M12 and 1 × M14, 60–80 Nm each.
  • 03Slowly raise the crane. Guide the engine upward, checking clearance with the AC compressor line and coolant pipes. The engine comes out vertically on the Verito — it does not tilt.
  • 04Transfer these items from the old engine to the new unit: alternator + bracket, AC compressor + bracket, power steering pump (if bolted to the engine), engine mount rubbers (use new ones if the rubber is cracked), and the starter motor if the replacement unit did not include one.
  • 05Compare the injector bodies: if old is Delphi and new is Bosch, note the difference — the wiring harness connector and ECU strategy will differ. Plan the ECU approach (see Phase 7) before lowering the new engine in.

Phase 05 — New Engine Installation
Lower In &
Torque Up
With the new K9K 85PS engine prepped with all ancillaries transferred, lower it into the Verito bay. Mount alignment is straightforward because the block design is identical. Use new engine mount rubbers for best NVH control.
  • 01Pre-fill the new engine's oil filter with clean 5W-40 and manually fill the turbo oil feed pipe with approximately 10ml of clean oil using a syringe before installation. This pre-lubricates the turbo bearing on first start.
  • 02Lower the engine slowly on the crane. The right-side torque mount bracket locates first — align its rubber mount with the subframe bracket before allowing the engine to settle.
  • 03Hand-thread all three engine mount bolts before torquing any of them. Then torque in sequence: lower subframe mount first (80 Nm), right torque mount (60 Nm), left bracket (60 Nm).
  • 04Re-mate the gearbox. Guide the input shaft into the clutch centre (rotate the gearbox input shaft slightly if it won't seat — it needs to align with the clutch splines). Torque bell-housing bolts to 40–50 Nm.
  • 05Refit both driveshafts. Always fit new inner oil seals on both sides — the old ones will leak oil into the gearbox if reused. Torque the lower ball joint pinch bolt (60 Nm) and hub nut (230 Nm + new split pin).
  • 06Refit all exhaust components with new gaskets and copper anti-seize on the studs. Never reuse old exhaust manifold gaskets.
Engine Hoist Torque Wrench (both ranges) Loctite 243 Copper Anti-Seize
Critical Check Do not torque the engine mount bolts in isolation. All three mount bolts must be hand-threaded before final torquing. If you torque one mount bolt with others not yet in position, the rubber bushing will be installed under stress and will wear prematurely.
Phase 06 — ECU, Wiring & Injector Coding
Electronics &
ECU Strategy
This is the most technically demanding phase. The K9K's common rail system requires each injector's unique correction code to be stored in the ECU. Without this, the engine will start but run rough, produce excess smoke, and set fault codes.
Option A — Donor ECU + Remap
Bring the entire donor ECU, keep the Verito harness and modify connector if needed. Have it remapped to the Verito's immobiliser code by a specialist.
✓ ECU is pre-matched to donor injectors
✓ Cleanest configuration
✓ Minimal fault code risk
✗ May need immobiliser re-coding
✗ Slightly more complex wiring if harness differs
Option B — Remap Existing ECU
Keep the Verito's existing ECU and remap it to 85PS calibration. Then enter new injector C2I codes via diagnostic tool.
✓ No immobiliser complications
✓ Simpler if injector types match
✗ Requires specialist with Renault Clip
✗ C2I coding still mandatory
✗ ECU must physically match ECU type
  • 01Reconnect all sensor plugs referencing your labelled connectors and photographs from Phase 02. The MAF, MAP, crank sensor, cam sensor, and coolant sensor are the most critical for starting.
  • 02Locate the injector C2I codes: each injector has a 16-character alphanumeric code laser-etched on its body. Write down all 4 codes before installing the injectors in the new engine.
  • 03Connect your Renault Clip or DDT2000 interface to the OBD port. Navigate to: Engine ECU → Injection → Injector Coding. Enter each injector's C2I code for cylinders 1–4 in the correct cylinder position.
  • 04Check glow plug resistance with the multimeter: each glow plug should read approximately 0.4–0.6 Ω. A reading of 0 Ω = short circuit. A reading of OL (open circuit) = failed glow plug — replace before first start.
  • 05Reconnect the battery. Cycle the ignition key ON–OFF three times to allow the fuel pump to prime and the ECU to begin self-calibration. Do not crank yet.
Renault Clip / DDT2000 OBD2 Scanner — Launch X431 Multimeter — Fluke
Do Not Skip Injector Coding Running the K9K with uncoded injectors causes injector washout, rough idle, white smoke, and potential catalytic damage. The C2I code corrects manufacturing tolerances for each injector's spray pattern. This step cannot be bypassed.
Phase 07 — Fluids, Ancillaries & First Start
Fill, Bleed
& Fire Up
With all connections made and injectors coded, refill all fluids systematically. The cooling system bleed procedure is critical — air pockets in the K9K coolant circuit cause overheating within minutes.
  • 01Fill the engine with 4.2 litres of 5W-40 full synthetic engine oil (e.g. Castrol Edge 5W-40 or Mobil 1 ESP). Check level on the dipstick — must read between min and max.
  • 02Fill the coolant system: use OAT (Organic Acid Technology) coolant mixed 50/50 with distilled water. Fill the expansion tank slowly, leaving the radiator bleed nipple open until coolant appears at the nipple, then close.
  • 03Reconnect the intercooler pipes and air intake. Ensure all clamps are tightened — a loose intercooler pipe means zero boost and a no-power symptom that is frustrating to diagnose.
  • 04Fuel prime procedure: turn ignition ON (no cranking) for 10 seconds, then OFF. Repeat 3 times. This runs the lift pump and fills the fuel rail. On the 4th cycle, crank the engine — it may take 5–8 seconds to start as the rail pressurises for the first time.
  • 05Once running: idle for 10 minutes. Watch the coolant temperature gauge — it should rise to operating temp (90°C) and stabilise. Watch for any oil pressure warning lamp — it must go out within 3 seconds of starting. Check underneath for oil or coolant leaks immediately.
  • 06Connect the OBD scanner and check for live data: fuel rail pressure should be 250–350 bar at idle, MAF should read 12–16 kg/hr at idle, no active fault codes (DTC). Clear any pending/stored codes from the old configuration.
Cooling System Pressure Tester OBD2 Scanner — Live Data Oil Drain Pan
Cooling Bleed Tip After initial idle, squeeze the top radiator hose 3–4 times to burp any remaining air pockets. Top up the expansion tank and re-check the coolant level after the engine has fully cooled down (next morning). Coolant typically drops 100–200ml as air purges.
Phase 08 — Break-In, Road Test & Long-Term Care
Break In &
Commission
The K9K is a proven engine with documented longevity exceeding 300,000 km — but this requires disciplined break-in and consistent servicing. The first 1000 km are critical for seating rings and bedding the turbo bearing.
  • 010–500 km: keep RPM below 3000, avoid sustained full throttle, do not exceed 100 km/h. Vary the engine speed — do not hold one RPM for long stretches. This beds the piston rings.
  • 02Change oil and oil filter at 500 km. This is mandatory — the oil will contain metal particles from the new engine's first seating. Drain hot, refill with 5W-40 full synthetic.
  • 03500–1000 km: gradually extend the RPM range. Try moderate acceleration runs up to 3500 rpm. Check for any developing leaks at the 500 km oil change — turbo oil feed banjo, sump gasket, coolant hoses.
  • 04At 1000 km: scan with the OBD tool again. Check that the ECU has learned adaptive fuel trims (short-term fuel trim should be within ±5%). The ECU takes approximately 800–1000 km to fully relearn the new engine's characteristics.
  • 05Ongoing: change oil every 10,000 km (not 20,000 km as stated for European conditions). Indian roads and fuel quality demand shorter intervals. Change the fuel filter every 20,000 km — the K9K common rail system is sensitive to contaminated fuel.
  • 06Timing belt: replace every 100,000 km. The K9K is an interference engine — a snapped belt bends all 8 valves. Budget for this job and do not defer it.

Turbo Protection After any long highway run, idle the engine for 2 minutes before switching off. This lets the turbo bearing oil cool and prevents coking. Fit an oil temp gauge if you do regular high-speed highway driving.
