What are the Ṣaḍ-Darśanas?

Darśana means "to see" or "a way of seeing reality." Long before smartphones and science labs, six groups of Indian sages sat under trees and asked the biggest questions imaginable — Who am I? What is real? How do I find lasting happiness? Their six answers became six schools that still influence millions of lives.

🔍
School 01
Nyāya
"The Logic Detectives"
👤 Founded by Sage Gautama

Nyāya is all about right reasoning & valid proof. These philosophers were like ancient lawyers and scientists — they believed you can only know something is true if you can prove it through perception, inference, comparison, or reliable testimony.

🧠 Critical Thinking ⚖️ Debate Club 🔬 Scientific Method
📖 The Story

Imagine you smell smoke but can't see fire. A Nyāya thinker says: "There MUST be fire, because smoke never exists without it." This is called Anumāna (inference). Sound familiar? It's exactly how Sherlock Holmes thinks — and how scientists form hypotheses! Sage Gautama wrote the Nyāya Sūtra, essentially the world's first textbook on logic.

🧒

Hey kids! Nyāya says: "Don't just believe — ask for PROOF!" Just like a good detective, always ask "How do you know that?"

⚛️
School 02
Vaiśeṣika
"The Atom Explorers"
👤 Founded by Sage Kaṇāda

Vaiśeṣika asks: what is everything made of? Sage Kaṇāda proposed that all matter is made of tiny, indestructible particles called paramāṇu — atoms! He said this 2,600 years before modern science confirmed it.

⚛️ Atomic Theory 🌌 Physics 🔭 Classification
📖 The Story

Kaṇāda's name literally means "atom eater" — legend says he was so obsessed with tiny particles that he'd pick up grain-sized bits of food to study them. He said reality has 9 substances: earth, water, fire, air, ether, time, space, mind, and soul. Modern physics has "fields and particles." Different words — remarkably similar obsession!

🧒

Did you know LEGO bricks are basically Vaiśeṣika philosophy? Everything in the universe is built from tiny pieces that combine in different ways!

🌊
School 03
Sāṃkhya
"The Great Mapper"
👤 Founded by Sage Kapila

Sāṃkhya is the oldest Indian philosophy and perhaps its most daring. It says reality has exactly two ingredients: Puruṣa (pure consciousness, the witness) and Prakṛti (nature, the performer). Suffering comes from confusing the two.

🧬 Evolution Theory 🎭 Mind-Body Split 🧘 Yoga Foundation
📖 The Story

Think of watching a movie. The screen (Puruṣa) never changes — it just lights up. The movie (Prakṛti) has drama, action, tears. We suffer when we forget we're the screen and think we ARE the movie. Modern mindfulness teachers say almost the same thing: "You are not your thoughts — you are the one who watches them." Sāṃkhya said it first!

🧒

Next time you feel angry or sad, try being the "camera operator" watching the feeling rather than being inside it. That's Sāṃkhya in action!

🧘
School 04
Yoga
"The Practice Masters"
👤 Systematised by Sage Patañjali

While Sāṃkhya made the map, Yoga gave the GPS and the vehicle. Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras is a 196-verse practical manual for training the mind — the world's oldest self-help book, and it still works perfectly today.

🏋️ Mental Training 🌍 300M+ practitioners 🧠 Neuroscience link
📖 The Story

Patañjali's opening line: "Yogaś-citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ" — Yoga is the stilling of the fluctuations of the mind. Modern neuroscientists at Harvard found that 8 weeks of meditation literally shrinks the amygdala (your brain's panic button) and thickens the prefrontal cortex (decision-making HQ). Patañjali described the same result 2,000 years ago — just without the brain scanner!

🧒

Try this: breathe in for 4 counts, hold 4, out for 4. Do it 3 times. Your mind just got a little more Yogic! 🌬️

📜
School 05
Mīmāṃsā
"The Duty Champions"
👤 Founded by Sage Jaimini

Mīmāṃsā asks: "What should we DO?" It says the Vedas are eternal, authorless truth and our highest purpose is to perform our duties (dharma) perfectly — without expecting rewards. It's the philosophy of commitment, ritual, and righteous action.

⚖️ Ethics & Law 🫡 Duty over Reward 📋 Constitutional Law
📖 The Story

Imagine a doctor who treats patients brilliantly whether they get famous or not — because it's the right thing to do. Or a soldier who protects citizens even when nobody is watching. That's Mīmāṃsā ethics. Remarkably, India's Supreme Court still references concepts from Mīmāṃsā in legal interpretation — this ancient school literally shaped modern Indian law!

🧒

Mīmāṃsā challenge: Do one kind thing today without telling ANYONE about it. That's the purest form of dharmic action! 🌟

✨
School 06
Vedānta
"The Grand Unifiers"
👤 Rooted in Bādarāyaṇa; perfected by Śaṅkara, Rāmānuja & Madhva

Vedānta — meaning "end of the Vedas" — is the crown jewel, exploring the deepest question: What is the ultimate nature of reality? Adi Śaṅkara's answer: Brahman alone is real; the world is appearance (māyā); you ARE Brahman.

🌌 Non-duality 🔬 Quantum parallels 🌍 Swami Vivekananda
📖 The Story

When Steve Jobs died, his last gift to attendees at his memorial was Paramahansa Yogananda's Autobiography of a Yogi — a pure Vedānta text. Erwin Schrödinger (quantum physics pioneer) kept the Upanishads on his desk and wrote that Vedānta's non-duality helped him conceive the idea of a universal wave function. "The total number of minds in the universe is one," he wrote. Śaṅkara smiled from 800 CE.

🧒

Vedānta's biggest idea: the same light shines through different coloured windows. The light is ONE — the colours are many. You and the stars are made of the same thing! ⭐

The Three Famous Pairs 👯

Indian tradition groups the six schools into three pairs — each pair shares common roots and complements each other perfectly.

🔍⚛️ Nyāya + Vaiśeṣika

The Scientists. Nyāya provides the logical tools (HOW to know), while Vaiśeṣika provides the content (WHAT to know about the universe). Together they form ancient India's philosophy of natural science. They eventually merged into Nyāya-Vaiśeṣika — the world's first unified theory of epistemology and physics.

🌊🧘 Sāṃkhya + Yoga

The Theorist + The Practitioner. Sāṃkhya is the map — a precise analysis of consciousness and matter. Yoga is the journey — practical techniques to experience what Sāṃkhya describes. You can't really do one without the other. The Bhagavad Gītā beautifully weaves them together.

📜✨ Mīmāṃsā + Vedānta

The Early Vedas + The Later Vedas. Mīmāṃsā focuses on the karma-kāṇḍa (action portion — rituals and duties). Vedānta focuses on the jñāna-kāṇḍa (knowledge portion — ultimate truth). In a beautiful arc, you begin with Mīmāṃsā (doing right action) and graduate to Vedānta (knowing your true nature).
