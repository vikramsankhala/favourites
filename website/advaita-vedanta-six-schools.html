The Six Schools (Ṣaḍ-Darśanas)

Darśana means "a way of seeing." Long before smartphones or science labs, six groups of Indian sages asked the biggest questions ever — What is real? Who am I? How do I find lasting peace? Their answers still shape science, law, yoga and mindfulness today.

🔍
School 01
Nyāya
"The Logic Detectives"
👤 Sage Gautama · ~550 BCE

Nyāya is India's school of logic and valid reasoning. These philosophers were the world's first professional arguers — they said you can only know something is true if you can prove it through one of four valid means: direct perception, inference, comparison, or reliable testimony.

🧠 Critical Thinking ⚖️ Debate 🔬 Scientific Method
📖 Story

You can't see fire on the mountain, but you see smoke. A Nyāya sage says: "Fire MUST be there — smoke never comes without fire." This is Anumāna (inference). It's exactly how Sherlock Holmes reasons — and precisely how modern scientists form hypotheses. Gautama's Nyāya Sūtra is the world's oldest textbook on logic.

🧒

Hey kids! Nyāya says: NEVER just believe — ask for PROOF! You're already a Nyāya thinker every time you ask "But how do you know that?"

⚛️
School 02
Vaiśeṣika
"The Atom Explorers"
👤 Sage Kaṇāda · ~600 BCE

Vaiśeṣika asks: what is everything made of? Sage Kaṇāda proposed that all physical matter is made of tiny, indestructible, indivisible particles called paramāṇu — atoms — nearly 2,600 years before Dalton's modern atomic theory.

⚛️ Atomic Theory 🌌 Physics 🔭 Categorisation
📖 Story

Kaṇāda's name literally means "grain eater" — legend says he was so obsessed with tiny particles he'd pick up and study the tiniest food fragments. He identified 9 substances making up reality: earth, water, fire, air, ether, time, space, soul and mind. Modern physics has "quantum fields and elementary particles." Different words; the same obsession!

🧒

LEGO bricks are basically Vaiśeṣika philosophy! Everything in the universe — your phone, a mountain, a star — is just tiny "bricks" combining in different ways. 🧱

🌊
School 03
Sāṃkhya
"The Great Mapper"
👤 Sage Kapila · ~700 BCE

Sāṃkhya is possibly the oldest Indian philosophy. It boldly claims that reality has exactly two ingredients: Puruṣa (pure consciousness — the unchanging witness) and Prakṛti (nature — the performer). Suffering arises when we forget which one we are.

🧬 Consciousness 🎭 Mind-Body 🧘 Yoga Base
📖 Story

Think of a cinema screen and a movie. The screen (Puruṣa) never changes — it's silent, still, pure light. The movie (Prakṛti) has drama, tears, explosions. We suffer when we forget we're the screen and think we ARE the movie. Every modern mindfulness teacher says the same: "You are not your thoughts — you watch them." Sāṃkhya said it first, ~2,700 years ago.

🧒

Next time you feel angry, try to be the "camera operator" noticing the anger — don't be inside it. Watch it like a movie. That IS Sāṃkhya! 🎬

🧘
School 04
Yoga
"The Practice Masters"
👤 Sage Patañjali · ~200 BCE

If Sāṃkhya is the map, Yoga is the GPS and the vehicle. Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras — 196 compact verses — is the world's oldest self-help manual. Its core teaching: Yoga is the stilling of the fluctuations of the mind (yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ).

🧠 Neuroscience 🌍 300M+ practitioners 🏋️ Mental Training
📖 Story

Harvard Medical School researchers found that 8 weeks of meditation literally shrinks the amygdala (the brain's alarm bell) and thickens the prefrontal cortex (the wise decision-maker). Patañjali described this exact transformation ~2,200 years ago — just without the MRI scanner! The Indian Army, Google, the NBA and NASA all use techniques rooted in his system.

🧒

Try it now: breathe IN for 4 counts… HOLD for 4… OUT for 4. Do it 3 times. Your brain just got calmer. Patañjali would be proud! 🌬️

📜
School 05
Mīmāṃsā
"The Duty Champions"
👤 Sage Jaimini · ~400 BCE

Mīmāṃsā asks: "What is the right thing to DO?" It says the Vedas are eternal truth, and our highest purpose is to perform our duties (dharma) flawlessly — without attachment to personal reward. It is the philosophy of commitment, ethics and righteous action.

⚖️ Ethics & Law 🫡 Duty over Reward 📋 Indian Law Basis
📖 Story

Imagine a doctor who gives exactly the same care to every patient whether they get famous for it or not — simply because healing is the right thing to do. That's Mīmāṃsā ethics in action. Remarkably, India's Supreme Court still references Mīmāṃsā principles in legal interpretation of Sanskrit texts. This ancient school literally underpins modern Indian law.

🧒

Mīmāṃsā challenge: Do one kind thing today WITHOUT telling a single person about it. No social media! That's pure dharmic action. 🌟

✨
School 06
Vedānta
"The Grand Unifiers"
👤 Bādarāyaṇa; perfected by Śaṅkara · ~788 CE

Vedānta — "end of the Vedas" — is the philosophical crown of India, exploring the deepest question: What is the ultimate nature of reality? Śaṅkara's Advaita answer: Brahman alone is real; the world is appearance (māyā); your true Self IS Brahman.

🌌 Non-duality 🔬 Quantum parallels 🌍 Vivekananda
📖 Story

When Steve Jobs died, his parting gift to guests at his memorial was Paramahansa Yogananda's Autobiography of a Yogi — pure Vedānta. Quantum physicist Erwin Schrödinger kept the Upanishads on his desk and wrote: "The total number of minds in the universe is one." Śaṅkara had written the same thing 1,200 years earlier.

🧒

Vedānta's big idea: the same sunlight shines through all different coloured windows. The light is ONE — the colours just look different. You, the stars, and everything are made of the same one thing! ⭐

The Three Classic Pairs 👯

🔍⚛️ Nyāya + Vaiśeṣika

The Scientists. Nyāya gives the tools of knowing (HOW to prove things); Vaiśeṣika gives the content (WHAT the universe is made of). Together they form India's ancient philosophy of natural science.

🌊🧘 Sāṃkhya + Yoga

Theory + Practice. Sāṃkhya is the blueprint — a precise analysis of consciousness and matter. Yoga is the construction — practical methods to live what Sāṃkhya describes.

📜✨ Mīmāṃsā + Vedānta

Action → Wisdom. Mīmāṃsā teaches you to act rightly. Vedānta reveals why — your true nature is the ultimate reality. Begin with duty; graduate to truth.

Ādi Śaṅkarācārya

The boy who became a monk at eight, debated the greatest scholars in India, wrote commentaries on all major scriptures — and changed the world — all before the age of 32.

⭐ THE MASTER OF ADVAITA

Ādi Śaṅkara

c. 788 – 820 CE · Kerala, India

Born in Kalady, Kerala, Śaṅkara showed extraordinary gifts from infancy. By age 8 he had memorised the Vedas and begged his widowed mother to let him become a renunciant (sannyāsi). Legend tells how a crocodile seized his leg in a river; he called out to his mother that he was dying — would she grant him renunciation? She did. The crocodile released him. He walked away from home forever.

He became the student of Govinda Bhagavatpāda on the banks of the Narmada, then embarked on a digvijaya (victory tour) of India on foot — from Kerala to Kashmir to Badrinath to Varanasi — debating every philosopher he met. He never lost a debate.

He established four monasteries (maṭhas) at the four corners of India — Sringeri, Dwarka, Puri, and Joshimath — institutions that still operate today, 1,200 years later.

He wrote commentaries on the Brahma Sūtras, all ten major Upanishads, and the Bhagavad Gītā — plus devotional hymns of astonishing beauty. His system is called Advaita Vedānta: non-dual wisdom.

Brahma satyam jagan mithyā, jīvo brahmaiva nāparaḥ — Brahman alone is real; the world is appearance; the individual self is none other than Brahman. — Śaṅkara, core teaching of Advaita Vedānta

Brahman

BRAHMAN · The Absolute Reality

Brahman is the one, infinite, undivided consciousness that is the only ultimate reality. It has no form, no limits, no beginning and no end. Everything that exists is Brahman — like waves that are always, entirely, nothing but ocean water.

🧒 Imagine one huge ocean. Every wave looks different but it's all the same water. Brahman is the ocean; you and me and the stars are all waves! 🌊

Ātman = Brahman

MAHĀVĀKYA · The Great Saying: "Aham Brahmāsmi"

The most radical claim in Advaita: your deepest Self (Ātman — the pure witness behind all your thoughts) is identical to Brahman. Not similar. Not connected. Identical. "I am Brahman." This is the highest discovery Vedānta points to.

🧒 The same sunshine that fills the whole sky also shines inside a little jar. The jar's light and the sky's light are THE SAME light. You are that! ☀️

Māyā

MĀYĀ · The Power of Appearance

Māyā is the inexplicable cosmic power that makes the one Brahman appear as a many-coloured, diverse world — like a magician's illusion, or like a rope mistaken for a snake in dim light. The world isn't fake; it just isn't ultimately real the way Brahman is.

🧒 In a dream, everything feels totally real — until you wake up! Māyā is like the dream. Brahman is the one who wakes up. 🌙➡️☀️

The Three Levels of Reality

PĀRAMĀRTHIKA · VYĀVAHĀRIKA · PRĀTIBHĀSIKA

Absolute (Brahman alone, ever-real) → Empirical (the everyday waking world, relatively real) → Apparent (dreams and illusions, momentarily real). Śaṅkara used this framework to show that the world isn't dismissed — it's placed in its proper context.

🧒 Gold jewellery is real as jewellery. But ultimately it's all just gold. The shapes are "vyāvahārika" real; the gold is "pāramārthika" real! 💍

Viveka, Vairāgya & Mumukṣutva

SĀDHANĀ CATUṢṬAYA · The Four Qualifications

Śaṅkara prescribed four preparations for Vedāntic enquiry: Viveka (discernment between real and unreal), Vairāgya (non-attachment to outcomes), Ṣaṭ-sampat (six virtues including calmness), and Mumukṣutva (burning desire for liberation). You need all four before the teaching truly lands.

🧒 It's like a smartphone needing both hardware AND software AND a charged battery. Vedānta needs a prepared mind — and YOU can prepare it! 📱

Vedānta Today

Advaita Vedānta isn't a museum piece. It is alive, practised and profoundly relevant — in hospitals, laboratories, boardrooms, therapy rooms and living rooms around the world.

🧬

Quantum Physics

Erwin Schrödinger (Nobel Prize, 1933) wrote that Vedāntic non-duality directly inspired his concept of a single universal wave-function. Werner Heisenberg studied the Upanishads. David Bohm's "implicate order" reads like a modern Advaita text. The parallels are too deep to be coincidental.

🔬 Science
🧠

Consciousness Studies

The "hard problem of consciousness" — why does subjective experience exist at all? — baffles modern neuroscience. Advaita answers: consciousness is primary, not produced by the brain. Researchers like David Chalmers and Bernardo Kastrup now seriously engage Vedāntic frameworks.

🧠 Neuroscience
🧘

Mindfulness & Therapy

MBSR (Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction), ACT therapy, and many modern wellbeing programmes are direct descendants of Vedāntic witness-consciousness practices. Thousands of hospitals worldwide prescribe them. Śaṅkara's "be the witness, not the witnessed" is now clinical medicine.

❤️ Wellbeing
🌍

Swami Vivekananda

In 1893, a 30-year-old monk from Calcutta walked onto the Parliament of World Religions stage in Chicago and said: "Sisters and Brothers of America…" — and the hall erupted. His Vedānta, rooted in Śaṅkara, gave the modern West its first real encounter with Indian philosophy and sparked a global spiritual revolution.

🌏 Global Impact
💼

Leadership & Business

Vedāntic concepts like nishkāma karma (action without selfish attachment) are now taught at IIMs, Harvard Business School and in executive coaching. Leaders like Satya Nadella (Microsoft CEO) cite Indian philosophy as central to their leadership approach.

💡 Leadership
📱

Silicon Valley & Tech

Steve Jobs famously gave every attendee at his memorial a Vedānta text. Apple's aesthetic philosophy of "the essential beneath the ornamental" echoes Śaṅkara's stripping away of māyā to reveal Brahman. The tech world's fascination with "non-duality" and "flow states" is Advaita repackaged.

💻 Technology

The Journey Through Time

From the earliest sages to the modern classroom — 3,500 years of living wisdom.

~1500 BCE

The Vedas Are Composed

The oldest scriptures of humanity — hymns, rituals and philosophical reflections — form the bedrock on which all six schools will later be built.

~800–600 BCE

The Upanishads

Forest sages record their insights on Ātman and Brahman in dialogues of astonishing depth. "Tat tvam asi" — That thou art. Vedānta's seed is planted.

~700–200 BCE

The Six Schools Emerge

Kapila, Gautama, Kaṇāda, Jaimini and Patañjali each systematise their insights into foundational sūtras. India's philosophical golden age blazes.

c. 200 BCE

Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras

The world's first complete manual on mental training. Still unrivalled. 300 million people practise its legacy today.

c. 788 CE

Śaṅkara Is Born

A child of extraordinary gifts appears in Kalady, Kerala. In 32 years he will traverse India on foot, debate every philosopher, write dozens of texts, and establish Advaita Vedānta as the crown of Indian thought.

c. 820 CE

Śaṅkara Passes On

At around 32, having changed the intellectual world of India forever, Śaṅkara is said to have walked into the Kedarnath peaks and merged with the infinite — fitting for someone who always taught that separation was the only illusion.

1893 CE

Vivekananda at Chicago

"Sisters and Brothers of America!" — three words that broke the ice of Western spiritual insularity. Vedānta goes global. The West would never look the same again.

Today

Vedānta Lives On

Studied at S-VYASA University, taught in hospitals as mindfulness, cited in quantum physics papers, quoted by tech CEOs. Śaṅkara's vision — that consciousness is the foundation of all reality — is more relevant now than ever.
